Instructions

Cells in the grid must be colored or left blank according to numbers at the side of the grid to reveal a hidden picture.
The numbers measures how many unbroken lines of filled-in squares there are in any given row or column.
For example, a clue of "3 2 1" would mean there are sets of three, two, and one filled squares, in that order, with at least one blank square between successive groups.
